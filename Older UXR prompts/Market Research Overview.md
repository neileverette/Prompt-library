

# Overview of the market research

Product design market research involves defining goals, identifying competitors (direct/indirect), analyzing their offerings (features, pricing, UX), understanding the market (trends, gaps), and auditing their strategies (content, SEO, social) using frameworks like SWOT, PEST, or Porter's Five Forces to find opportunities and threats, ultimately informing your design strategy. Best practices include asking the right questions, focusing on top competitors, interviewing their users, and using a systematic approach to uncover actionable insights.

## The "Why" & "Who"

1.  **Define Objectives:**  What do you need to learn? (e.g., market size, customer needs, trends).
2.  **Industry Outlook:**  Understand the overall market, growth, regulations (PEST analysis: Political, Economic, Social, Technological).
3.  **Target Customer:**  Who are they? What are their pain points? What solutions do they use?.
4.  **Market Gaps:**  Where are unmet needs or underserved segments?.

## The "What" & "How"

1.  **Identify Competitors:**  Direct (same solution) & Indirect (different solution for same problem).
2.  **Set Goals for Audit:**  What specific features, strategies, or designs are you comparing?.
3.  **Analyze Competitor Offerings:**
    -   **Product:**  Features, UX/UI, performance, value proposition.
    -   **Price:**  Models, tiers, perceived value.
    -   **Place (Distribution):**  Channels, accessibility.
    -   **Promotion (Marketing):**  Messaging, channels, content, SEO.
4.  **Conduct Usability & Functional Tests:**  Experience their product as a user.
5.  **Interview Competitors' Customers:**  Get firsthand feedback on what works/doesn't for them.
