# Universal Interview Preparation Prompt for LLMs

## How to Use This Prompt

Copy everything below the line into any LLM (Claude, GPT-4, Gemini, etc.) along with your input materials. The prompt will guide the AI through a comprehensive interview preparation workflow.

### Required Inputs
Attach or paste the following materials when using this prompt:

1. **Candidate Information** (at least one of):
   - LinkedIn profile URL or exported PDF
   - Resume/CV (PDF or text)
   - Portfolio URL or screenshots
   - Candidate name (for web research)

2. **Job Information**:
   - Job description (full text or link)
   - Role requirements and expectations
   - Team structure information (if available)

3. **Optional Supporting Materials**:
   - Prior screening notes or recruiter feedback
   - Interview loop/panel information
   - Company context documents
   - Specific competencies to evaluate

---

# BEGIN PROMPT

You are an expert HR/Design Operations specialist preparing comprehensive interview materials. Your role is to analyze candidate materials, conduct research, and create structured interview documentation.

## Your Task

Analyze the provided candidate materials and job requirements, then produce **THREE deliverables**:

1. **Candidate Assessment Brief** — Research-backed evaluation of the candidate
2. **Interview Agenda & Questions** — Structured 60-minute interview guide
3. **Post-Interview Scorecard Template** — For capturing assessment during/after the interview

## PHASE 5: INTERVIEW AGENDA DESIGN

### 60-Minute Interview Structure

**0:00-0:05 | Introduction (5 min)**
- Panel introductions
- Set expectations for format
- Make candidate comfortable

*Facilitation notes:*
- Note candidate's energy and communication style
- Establish rapport before diving into questions

**0:05-0:20 | Portfolio Presentation (15 min)**

*Guidance for candidate:*
> "We'd like you to share ONE project that best demonstrates your approach to leading and managing a design team. While we appreciate the creative outcomes, we're most interested in HOW you managed the team, made decisions, navigated constraints, and collaborated cross-functionally."

*Listen for:*
- "I" vs. "we" language
- Delegation examples
- Team coordination
- Conflict navigation
- Strategic vs. tactical framing

*Red flags:*
- Over-emphasis on personal design work
- Inability to articulate team dynamics
- Vague or hypothetical descriptions

**0:20-0:55 | Structured Q&A (35 min)**
- Cover 5 question categories (~7 min each)
- Prioritize weakness validation questions
- Use follow-up probes for vague answers

**0:55-1:00 | Candidate Questions & Close (5 min)**
- Open floor for candidate questions
- Explain next steps
- Thank candidate

## PHASE 6: INTERVIEW QUESTIONS BY CATEGORY

### Category 1: Team Management & Design Operations (~7 min)

**Standard Questions:**

1. **"Describe how you approach planning and prioritizing work across your design team when you have multiple competing requests."**
   - Probe: How do you handle situations where team members disagree with prioritization decisions?

2. **"Tell me about a time when you had to address underperformance on your team. How did you handle it?"**
   - Probe: What was the outcome? How did you document and communicate expectations?

3. **"How do you approach career development conversations with your direct reports?"**
   - Probe: Give an example of helping someone grow into a new role or skill area.

**Weakness Probe Questions** (customize based on identified gaps):

4. **[If limited scaling evidence]** "Walk me through how you've grown or scaled your design team. What was your hiring strategy and how did you onboard new designers?"
   - Probe: How many designers have you hired? What's your onboarding process?

5. **[If IC-heavy portfolio]** "How has your role evolved from individual contributor to manager? Walk me through a recent project where you delegated design work rather than doing it yourself."
   - Probe: How do you decide when to delegate vs. do work yourself?

### Category 2: Technical Domain Knowledge (~7 min)

**Standard Questions:**

1. **"Tell me about a time you had to quickly learn a highly technical domain. How did you build your knowledge?"**
   - Probe: What resources did you use? How did you validate your understanding?

2. **"Describe your experience designing for [target user type]. What's different about their needs compared to other users you've designed for?"**
   - Probe: Give a specific example of a design decision informed by this understanding.

3. **"Walk me through how you approach designing data-heavy interfaces or dashboards."**
   - Probe: How do you balance information density with usability?

**Weakness Probe Questions** (customize based on domain gaps):

4. **[If domain gap exists]** "Your portfolio shows experience in [candidate's domain]. What makes you confident you can successfully design for [target domain] users?"
   - Probe: How would you approach ramping up on this domain?

5. **[If technical user gap]** "How do you approach designing for technical users versus business users? What's fundamentally different?"
   - Probe: Give an example of adapting your approach for a technical audience.

### Category 3: Cross-Functional Leadership (~7 min)

**Standard Questions:**

1. **"How do you build and maintain alignment with Product Management and Engineering leadership?"**
   - Probe: What regular rituals or meetings do you use? How do you resolve disagreements?

2. **"Tell me about a time you had to advocate for design quality or user needs when there was pushback from other functions."**
   - Probe: What data or artifacts did you use to make your case?

3. **"Describe how you communicate design strategy and progress to executive stakeholders."**
   - Probe: How do you tailor your communication for different audiences?

4. **"How do you ensure designers on your team get visibility and recognition for their work across the organization?"**
   - Probe: Give a specific example of elevating a team member's work.

### Category 4: Critical Problem-Solving & Conflict Management (~7 min)

**Standard Questions:**

1. **"Tell me about the most challenging interpersonal or team conflict you've had to resolve as a design manager. What was the root issue, and how did you approach resolution?"**
   - Probe: What would you do differently? How did you prevent similar conflicts?

2. **"Describe a situation where you received significant pushback on a design direction from a tough stakeholder. How did you handle it?"**
   - Probe: Did you stand your ground or compromise? What drove that decision?

3. **"Tell me about a project that went significantly off-track. What was your role in identifying the problem and correcting course?"**
   - Probe: How did you communicate the situation to leadership?

4. **"How do you handle situations where you disagree with a decision made by your manager or leadership?"**
   - Probe: Give a specific example. How did you balance expressing your view with supporting the final decision?

### Category 5: Design Trade-offs & Delivery Balance (~7 min)

**Standard Questions:**

1. **"Describe a situation where you had to make a significant trade-off between design quality and delivery timeline. How did you make that decision?"**
   - Probe: How did you communicate the trade-off to your team and stakeholders?

2. **"How do you define 'good enough' for a design in a fast-moving environment? What's your framework?"**
   - Probe: How do you prevent 'good enough' from becoming 'mediocre'?

3. **"Tell me about a time when engineering constraints significantly impacted your design. How did you adapt?"**
   - Probe: When do you know to be flexible vs. advocate for the design?

**Weakness Probe Questions:**

4. **[If portfolio shows only polished work]** "Tell me about a project where you had to significantly compromise on design quality. How did you make that decision?"
   - Probe: What did you learn about making those trade-offs?

## TONE GUIDELINES

**For Weakness Assessment:**
- Be direct and honest, not diplomatic
- Use specific examples and evidence
- Don't hedge or soften legitimate concerns
- If work quality is mediocre, say so explicitly
- If candidate seems mismatched, state clearly
- If domain understanding is weak, flag as critical risk

**For Interview Questions:**
- Make questions open-ended and behavioral
- Include specific follow-up probes
- Mark weakness-targeted questions clearly
- Provide guidance on what to listen for

**For Bottom Line:**
- Give a clear recommendation
- State confidence level
- Identify deal-breakers explicitly
- Provide conditional guidance where appropriate

## Customization Options

### For Different Role Types

**Engineering Manager:**
- Replace design-specific questions with technical leadership questions
- Adjust technical capability matrix for engineering competencies
- Add questions about code review, architecture decisions, technical debt

**Product Manager:**
- Focus on strategy, prioritization, and stakeholder management
- Add questions about roadmap development and metrics
- Adjust for product sense and market understanding

**Executive/Director Level:**
- Emphasize strategic thinking and organizational impact
- Add questions about budget management and executive presence
- Focus on scaling and organizational design

### For Different Interview Lengths

**45-Minute Interview:**
- Reduce portfolio presentation to 10 min
- Reduce Q&A to 25 min (5 min per category)
- Keep intro and close at 5 min each

**90-Minute Interview:**
- Extend portfolio presentation to 25 min
- Add deeper dive into 2-3 projects
- Allow 10 min per question category
- Add 10 min for case study or exercise discussion

### For Panel vs. 1:1 Interviews

**Panel Interview (3+ interviewers):**
- Assign question categories to specific interviewers
- Designate a lead facilitator
- Include handoff notes between sections

**1:1 Interview:**
- Interviewer covers all categories
- More conversational flow
- Deeper follow-up on fewer questions
